A Welcome from our Artistic Director

It is with an immense sense of privilege, and indeed humility, that I take this opportunity to welcome you — each and every one of you — to what promises to be yet another extraordinary season here at Enyold Theatre. As an institution, we have always believed — and I think this is something that sets us apart — that the arts are not merely a luxury, nor simply an entertainment, but rather a fundamental and irreducible component of a truly flourishing civic life. That is the animating conviction behind everything we do here.

This season, more than any other, we are delighted to be presenting a programme that reflects the full breadth and diversity of what it means to live, to feel, and to experience the world in all its complexity. From the intimate and the personal to the epic and the transformational, our stages will once again play host to work that challenges, that moves, that provokes, and — yes — that sometimes simply delights. Because delight, too, has its place in the artistic canon.

I am particularly excited — and I say this with full awareness of how often artistic directors claim to be excited about things — to introduce a cohort of new voices and emerging practitioners alongside our established relationships with makers of genuine and lasting significance. The dialogue between the new and the known is, I believe, one of the most generative tensions available to a producing house such as ours, and this season we lean into it with, I hope, some courage.

I want also to take this moment to thank you personally. Your continued support — whether you are joining us for the first time or returning for the twentieth consecutive season — is quite literally what makes all of this possible. The arts do not exist in a vacuum. They exist because people choose to show up, to be present, to give their attention. And your attention — in an age so aggressively competing for it — is genuinely the most generous gift you can give.

I look forward enormously to welcoming you into our spaces over the coming months. Do feel free to share your thoughts and reflections with us — we are, as ever, listening. Until then, I hope this season brings you something you were not expecting. That is, after all, what the best art always does.

— Rosalind Hatherley-Vane, Artistic Director, Enyold Theatre

How to get here

Public Transport›

We are within easy walking distance of the nearest bus and railway station and within a five minute drive of the motorway. Based in the town centre, we're just opposite the old Woolworths (you know the one — currently a mattress warehouse).     An Evening of Music & Things

    Main House · Saturdays · From £18

    We are delighted to present what promises to be one of the most memorable evenings in Enyold's recent programmatic history. This remarkable programme brings together a constellation of talent that, we feel, truly needs no introduction — and yet we shall provide one, in the interests of completeness.

    The evening will comprise a carefully curated selection of musical works, interspersed with other things. The precise nature of these things has not yet been entirely confirmed at time of going to print, however audiences can expect to be moved, challenged, and on occasion surprised.

    Drawing on a rich tapestry of influences that span the full breadth of the Western cannon, this evenings performances will demonstrate once again why Enyold remains at the vanguard of accessible yet uncompromising arts presentaion. We are honoured to welcome back several artists whose relationship with this building goes back many years, alongside exciting new collaboraters who bring fresh perspectives to familar forms.

    Tickets are available at multiple price points. A booking fee applies. Latecomers will not be admitted after the performance has commenced. Please arrive early and allow time for security.

    Performance (Main House)

    Main House · Various dates · From £22

    A new work. This Performance (working title) explores themes of presence, absence, and the relationship between performer and audience in a contemporary context.

    The company have been in residency at Enyold for several weeks now, developing material in response to the building and its history. What emerges is a peice that resists easy categorization — part installation, part durational performance, part something else entirely. Audiences are encouraged to move freely throught the space, although this is not mandatory.

    Content information: this work contains extended periods of silence. It also contains sound. There may be audience participation, depending on the night. Running time is variable. On some nights the performance may end before the advertised time. On others it may continue somewhat beyond it. There are no refunds in either eventuality.

    This production is supported by Arts Council England, Enyold Borough Council, and an anonymous donor who wishes to remain anonymous.

    Dance

    Main House · June 28 · From £24

    A dance production. This work brings together an ensemble of performers to explore movement, space, and — above all — the deeply human imperative to move one's body in time with, or occasionally against, music.

    The choreography in this piece has been described by the choreografer themselves as "intentional". It draws on traditions of contemporary dance while simultaneously questioning those very traditions — a tension that manifests most powerfully, perhaps, in the work's extraordinary second section, during which nothing happens for approximately eleven minutes. This has divided audiences. Some have found it profound. Others have asked for a refund. Both responses, the company feel, are valid.

    Running time: 1hr 10min approximately (no interval). Please note: there is no interval. Age guidance: suitable for all ages, though children under 5 may find certain sections difficult to follow, as indeed may some adults. The production contains strobe lighting and a section of extremely loud noise that has been described as music.
